  • Hi, I'm Ayishatu

    Ayishatu Saeed is a second-year student of the African Leadership College. She is studying computing who has taken relevant courses such as Web Development, Database Development and Network Technology courses. She is a Ghanaian by nationality

  • ALU Journey

    I discovered ALU while seeking for a university that encourages students to become entrepreneurs and is more mission-driven than major-driven. This also fits with how I might accomplish my goal and challenge myself in my leadership growth.

    The lectures at ALU challenge the coventional model of education. The cutting-edge learning methodology fills the gap between the classroom and the actual world. Though ALU is an international school, the application was not challenging. My dedication and determination allowed me to successfully complete the application process. My goal was to get accepted to ALU. ALU encourages students to take up difficult tasks and this gave me hope that I would be able to handle challenging situations.

    Moving to ALU was both exhilarating and nerve-wracking. I was anticipating attending my ideal college and traveling abroad for the first time, which makes me both anxious and eager. It made me consider how I might do without my family in different environment and one of my fears was leaving my mother behind in my native country.

    Diversity is alwalys valued at ALU. I was able to embrace and interrelate with other African students better as a result of this. At ALU, inever felt lonely because of the positive environment that unites us together.

    It was incredible to interact with students from other nationals in the class. We frequently organize into peer groups, work together on projects and have fun. Some of the projects were focused on customer needs, and we worked together as consultants to assist companies in creating business plans that would set them apart from their competitors. This exposure helped me with my presentation and organisational abilities during my internship.

    My counsel to prospective students is to pursue their passion, work hard at it, and exceed expectations. It's not essentially around majors but about completing the mission

  • Experience in Mauritius

    Mauritius is an amazing country to visit because of its beautiful environment, which includes beaches, mountains and other natural places. Getting to the shopping centre with a buddy, going to the beaches among others are a few of the foremost exciting viewpoints of being in Mauritius.

    Due to visiting and touring through the exquisite island of Mauritius, I have a developed a significant interest in tourism. I have learned around the locals' traditional values and beliefs and connected with some of them. I became intrigued about tourism and needed to build a platform that would let both travelers and local people connect together and appreciate Ghana's tourist attractions.
