  • Hi, I'm Yvette

    I am from from Rwanda, Western Province, Rubavu District. I am currently in my second year of a bachelor's degree in computing at the African Leadership College in Mauritius.

    Before coming to ALU, I went Lycée Notre Dame de Cîteaux In Kigali, Rwanda, and graduated with a High School Certificate.

  • ALU Journey

    I first learned about ALU when ALU alumni students from the Rwanda Campus visited my high school to inform us more about it and encourage us to apply.I first learned about ALU when ALU alumni students from Rwanda Campus visited my high school to encourage us to apply. I wanted to study at ALU because I believed that they adopt an innovative strategy in which students learn largely by practicing in the actual world. I applied to pursue a Bachelor's of Computing in Mauritius and was admitted, which excited me. In January 2021, I traveled to ALU Mauritius. It was wonderful to meet other students. In level 1, I enrolled in the Leadership Core Program.

    My advice to prospective students is to set high personal and intellectual expectations for themselves and to live up to them. My university experiences thus far have been amazing. I've learnt and experienced so much in such a short period of time. ALU is a wonderfully inviting environment, and I felt right at home from the start.

  • Experience in Mauritius

    My experience of Mauritius as a place, the island is relatively safe — violent crimes are almost unheard of.

  • Social Life

    I had eaten most of my meals at restaurants for two years and worked a lot of hours in front of a computer and took little time for social life not to mention.

  • Ideas for the Future

    I want to become a professional Website developer. I would also like to work while pursuing a bachelor's degree in computing, so that I may contribute even more value to the organization for which I will be working.
